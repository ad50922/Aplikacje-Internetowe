Global warming, a significant aspect of climate change, refers to the long-term increase in Earth's average surface temperature. Predominantly a result of human activities, this rise in temperature poses severe threats to ecosystems, human health, and economies worldwide.

Climate change, particularly global warming, is the gradual elevation of Earth's surface temperature over time. Largely driven by human-induced factors, this climatic shift jeopardizes natural habitats, endangers human well-being, and has profound implications for global economic stability.

The phenomenon of global warming, an alarming facet of climate change, denotes the consistent rise in Earth's mean surface temperature. Largely attributable to human actions, this warming trend has far-reaching consequences for biodiversity, human safety, and the world's economic structures.
